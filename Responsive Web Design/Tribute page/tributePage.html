About Yip Man

  • Ip was born to Ip Oi-dor and Ng Shui as the third of four children. He grew up in a wealthy family in Foshan, Guangdong, and received traditional Chinese education, alongside his elder brother Ip Kai-gak, elder sister Ip Wan-mei and younger sister Ip Wan-hum.
  • Ip started learning Wing Chun from Chan Wah-shun when he was 9 or 13. Chan was 57 at the time, and Ip became Chan's 16th and last student.
  • At the age of 16, with help from his relative Leung Fut-ting, Ip moved to Hong Kong and there he attended school at St. Stephen's College, a secondary school for wealthy families and foreigners living in Hong Kong.
  • In 1917 when he was 24 and became a police officer there. He taught Wing Chun to several of his subordinates, friends and relatives, but did not officially run a martial arts school.
  • Ip died on 2 December 1972 in his unit at 149 Tung Choi Street in Hong Kong, from throat cancer, only seven months before the death of Bruce Lee. He was buried at Wo Hop Shek, Hong Kong
